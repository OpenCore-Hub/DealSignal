# DealSignal Go-To-Market Plan

## 1. GTM Thesis

DealSignal should start with fundraising founders, then expand into investment firms and B2B sales teams.

The wedge is strongest with founders because:

- Pain is urgent during fundraising.
- Buying decision is fast.
- The product naturally spreads to investors through shared links.
- Founders later become sales users as their companies grow.
- Investor exposure creates a path into VC and fund workflows.

Primary beachhead:

> Seed and Series A founders actively fundraising in the next 90 days.

Secondary expansion:

- VC funds and angels who receive DealSignal links.
- B2B sales teams at founder-led startups.
- Fund IR and M&A teams once Deal Rooms mature.

## 2. Market Segmentation

### Segment A: Fundraising Founders

ICP:

- Pre-seed to Series B startups
- 2-100 employees
- Actively fundraising or preparing to fundraise
- Founder-led sales and fundraising motion
- Uses Google Slides, PDF, Dropbox, DocSend, Notion, or Drive today

Pain triggers:

- Starting a fundraise
- Sending first pitch deck
- Investors ask for data room
- Existing DocSend feels expensive or high-friction
- Founder does not know who is serious

Core message:

> Know which investors are truly interested before your next follow-up.

Offer:

- Free fundraising deck tracker
- Investor intent dashboard
- Seed data room template
- 14-day fundraising sprint trial

### Segment B: Investment Firms

ICP:

- Emerging VC funds
- Micro funds
- PE / growth teams with smaller ops teams
- Fund IR teams sending LP updates
- M&A advisors running lightweight diligence rooms

Pain triggers:

- Raising a new fund
- Sending LP updates
- Running diligence
- Managing buyer access
- Need auditability without heavyweight VDR cost

Core message:

> Make LP and deal materials secure, branded, and measurable.

Offer:

- Branded LP update portal
- Lightweight deal room
- LP engagement dashboard
- Audit-ready access logs

### Segment C: B2B Sales Teams

ICP:

- SaaS companies with 5-100 sales reps
- Founder-led sales teams becoming structured sales orgs
- High-consideration sales cycles
- Deals involve multiple stakeholders
- Proposal, security, and ROI materials affect close rates

Pain triggers:

- Proposals disappear after being sent
- Champion forwards materials internally but seller lacks visibility
- CRM lacks engagement signals
- Sales content is inconsistent or outdated

Core message:

> Turn every proposal into a live buying-intent signal.

Offer:

- Proposal tracker
- Deal intent score
- Slack alerts for buying committee activity
- HubSpot / Salesforce sync

## 4. Product-Led Growth Loop

Loop:

1. Sender creates Smart Link.
2. Recipient opens a professional viewer.
3. Viewer shows subtle DealSignal branding.
4. Recipient experiences low-friction secure access.
5. Recipient later needs to send sensitive materials.
6. Recipient signs up using contextual CTA.

Recipient CTA examples:

- "Share your own deck with DealSignal"
- "Create a secure proposal link"
- "Start a deal room"

Important constraint:

Branding must never feel intrusive inside sensitive investor or enterprise workflows.

## 9. Competitive Positioning

Against DocSend:

- Lower recipient friction
- Segment-specific workflows
- Action recommendations, not just analytics
- Transparent data ownership
- Stronger founder and sales messaging

Against traditional VDRs:

- Faster setup
- Better UX
- Lower cost
- Better for lightweight fundraising, LP updates, and sales rooms

Against Dropbox / Google Drive:

- Recipient analytics
- Intent scoring
- Access audit
- Deal-specific workflows

- Better deal room support
- Simpler external sharing

## 16. Risks and Mitigations

Risk: Users perceive tracking as creepy.

Mitigation:

- Transparent recipient privacy language.
- Focus messaging on professional engagement, not surveillance.

Risk: Security promises are overinterpreted.

Mitigation:

- Be explicit that screenshots cannot be fully prevented.
- Position watermark and access control as deterrence and traceability.

Risk: Founder market is seasonal and episodic.

Mitigation:

- Expand from fundraising to investor updates and sales proposals.

Risk: DocSend owns category awareness.

Mitigation:

- Use comparison SEO, founder-specific positioning, and lower-friction viewer as wedge.

Risk: Enterprise security requirements slow adoption.

Mitigation:

- Start with founders and emerging funds before heavy enterprise procurement.
